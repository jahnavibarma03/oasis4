Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi

Born

2 October 1869 Porbandar, Porbandar State, Kathiawar Agency, British India

Died

30 January 1948 (aged 78) New Delhi, Dominion of India

A Journey of Light and Change

Mahatma Gandhi, fondly known as Gandhiji, was a beacon of truth, nonviolence, and selfless service. His life and teachings continue to inspire and guide generations around the world. Let us pay tribute to this extraordinary soul:

In a world of turmoil, he stood for peace,
A symbol of love that would never cease.
Gandhiji, with his spinning wheel in hand,
Showed us the power of principles to withstand.

Nonviolence, his weapon of choice,
He taught us to rise above anger's voice.
Through marches and protests, he led the way,
With truth as his armor, he'd never sway.

Simple living and high thinking he preached,
Inequality and injustice he vehemently breached.
From Dandi to Quit India, he led the fight,
For India's freedom, he shone so bright.

His life was a testament to inner strength,
A journey of self-discovery, at great length.
With humility and determination as his guide,
He transformed hearts and turned the tide.

Gandhiji's legacy lives on today,
In every heart that strives to find a way,
To solve conflicts without shedding blood,
And stand for justice, like he always stood.

Let's remember the man who walked his talk,
Whose legacy continues to forever stalk.
In a world often torn by hate and strife,
Gandhiji's message remains our guiding light.

Jai Hind
